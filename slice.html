One gesture drives all three. The slider is the position of an object from the next dimension up, sliding through the world below it. What you keep is only the slice that touches your space — never the whole shape.

Rucker's point, in one move: a being one dimension below you could never see your body — only the cross-sections that pass through their world as you push through it. The tesseract in panel 03 is doing to you exactly what the cube in panel 01 does to A. Square. The slice is all either of you ever gets.
